How to Choose and Maintain Your European Car

European cars offer an amazing blend of performance, comfort and technology. Whether you’re considering a BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Porsche, Mini or Volvo, choosing the right vehicle—and maintaining it properly—can save you time and money over the long run.

How to Choose the Right European Vehicle

1. Research Common Issues

Before you buy, research known issues for the model and year you’re considering. Some engines or transmissions may have specific weaknesses that you’ll want to know about in advance.

2. Check Maintenance History

A well-documented service history is a strong sign that a European car has been cared for properly. Look for regular oil changes, cooling system service and other scheduled maintenance.

3. Get a Pre-Purchase Inspection

A pre-purchase inspection by a European specialist like Dieter’s Motors in Oxnard can help uncover hidden issues before you commit to buying a vehicle.

How to Maintain Your European Car

  • Follow factory maintenance intervals
  • Use the correct European-spec oils and fluids
  • Replace filters on schedule
  • Inspect brakes, tires and suspension regularly
  • Address warning lights immediately
  • Stay ahead of cooling system and timing component wear
